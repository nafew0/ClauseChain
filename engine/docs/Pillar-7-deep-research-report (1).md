## Negative findings

- **No economy × indicator cell was wholly empty.** Each of Singapore, Malaysia and Australia has qualifying evidence for P7-I1 through P7-I5.
- **Singapore, P7-I4 — no general statutory DPIA duty found.** I checked the PDPA, Personal Data Protection Regulations 2021 and breach-notification regulations. Regulations 14 and 15 require assessments only for two specific legal bases. The all-sector accountability mechanism remains the DPO duty, so the likely indicator result is **1 because of DPO**, not because of a universal DPIA requirement.
- **Malaysia, P7-I2 — no all-entity operational cybersecurity duty found.** Act 854 creates a national governance structure, but its substantive assessment, audit and incident duties centre on NCII entities and licensed service providers. Under the rubric’s strict “dedicated but sectoral-only” limb, this supports **0.5** rather than 0.
- **Malaysia, P7-I4 — no unconditional DPO duty for every data controller or processor found.** The 2025 circular uses processing-volume and systematic-monitoring thresholds. The 2026 DPIA guideline applies to likely high-risk processing rather than every processing operation.
- **Australia, P7-I4 — no economy-wide private-sector DPO or DPIA obligation found.** I checked the Privacy Act 1988, the December-2024 reform Act and the Australian Government Agencies APP Code. The binding officer/PIA duties apply to Commonwealth agencies; s. 33D is direction-based. This supports **0.5**, not 1.
- **Australia, P7-I3 — no health-record localization rule found in the NSW or Victorian health-record provisions checked.** Both Acts impose retention floors, not a requirement that records be stored physically in the State or Australia.
